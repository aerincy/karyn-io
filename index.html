Strategic Impact & Case Studies

The Cape Canaveral Contract Turnaround

Interim Program Director  |  Space Florida IFM Contract  |  Kennedy Space Center & Cape Canaveral Space Force Station
Challenge:
A critical Integrated Facility Management (IFM) prime contract held by Space Florida was failing during a leadership transition, jeopardizing client trust, operational continuity, and a multi-million-dollar federal account. The contract's expansive scope covered the operations and maintenance of multiple high-profile aerospace facilities across both Kennedy Space Center and Cape Canaveral Space Force Station, including: Exploration Park at KSC (200-acre campus), Space Life Science Laboratory (104,000 sq. ft. facility), South Campus at CCSFS, Shuttle Landing Facility (15,000 LF runway and associated structures), Operational Storage Facility at Camp Blanding, and Area 57 at CCSFS (high bay processing facilities).
Action:
Deployed as Interim Program Director (Jan–Apr 2024) to stabilize the contract during a critical leadership transition. Coordinated directly with Space Florida's Facilities Manager to maintain operations, restructured the operational team, implemented rigorous compliance protocols, and rebuilt the client relationship through transparent, consistent stakeholder engagement. Navigated a multi-tier federal agreement environment including NASA Enhanced Use Lease Agreements (KSA series), USAF Property Licenses (AFSPC-DBEH series), and a Commercial Space Operations Agreement between USAF and Space Florida.
Result:
Stabilized all operations with zero disruptions to active aerospace infrastructure across KSC, CCSFS, and all associated sites. Restored full client trust and successfully retained the multi-million-dollar contract against competing national firms. Transitioned operations to permanent leadership upon stabilization.
